The little owl (Athene noctua)

This little owl often lives near villages and farmlands, likes to look at the windows of houses at night.

The call of a little owl was thought to have heralded the murder of Julius Caesar.

Closely associated with the Greek goddess Athena and the Roman goddess Minerva, and hence represents wisdom and knowledge.

  • Binomial name: Athene noctua
  • Length: 23-28 cm
  • Wingspan: 57-64 cm for both sexes
  • Weight: 150-200g
  • Distribution and habitat: is widespread across Europe, Asia and North Africa.

This little owl is most active at dusk, but also partly active by day, and at night. Often roosts by day in dense foliage or openings of holes. When disturbed at roost, it adopts a slim, upright position, then bobs its body up and down. If still threatened, it will fly away, or withdraw into a nearby hole. Avoids forest.

Feed on insects. Surplus food is stored in caches, generally in holes.

Little Owls may reach an age of about 16 years, but normally do not live that long. Many individuals are hit by vehicles when hunting at night. Severe winters with a lot of snow may also lead to huge losses in some populations.
